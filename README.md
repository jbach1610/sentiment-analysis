## üß† Methodology
This project combines **qualitative close reading** with **quantitative NLP techniques**:

### Quantitative Tools
- **Voyant Tools** for word frequency, dispersion, and trend visualization
- **Lexicon-based sentiment analysis** using:
  - Bing (binary positive/negative)
  - AFINN (valence intensity scoring)
  - NRC (emotion categories such as trust, sadness, joy)

### Qualitative Frameworks
- Negativity Bias (Cognitive Psychology)
- Self-Compassion Theory (Kristin Neff)
- Attachment Theory (Bowlby)

Selected segments were analyzed across **secure vs. insecure attachment relationships**
(e.g., family members vs. nurturing figures) to compare emotional language patterns.

## üìä Key Findings
- Positive and trust-oriented language appears **more frequently than expected**, despite
the novel‚Äôs traumatic context.
- The protagonist consistently uses **low-intensity negative words**, suggesting emotional
regulation rather than suppression.
- Language associated with **trust and compassion** often outweighs sadness, even in
interactions with abusive figures.
- The absence of extreme negative terms (e.g., ‚Äúhate‚Äù) highlights the role of
**self-compassion in mitigating negativity bias**.

These findings challenge surface-level interpretations of the novel and reveal its broader
emotional complexity.
